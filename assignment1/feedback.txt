I think this assignment is a good introduction to computational graphs and reverse-mode automatic differentiation.
The overall difficulty is moderate: the element-wise operators are straightforward, while matrix multiplication gradients
with transpose flags are the most time-consuming and error-prone part.

The notebook explanation is clear overall. One place that could use a bit more detail is the backward rule for `matmul`
under different transpose combinations, because that is where most implementation mistakes are likely to happen.

I also like that the assignment naturally supports checking higher-order gradients once the graph construction is correct.
