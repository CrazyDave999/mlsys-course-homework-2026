In this assignment I learned how a small automatic differentiation engine is built from a computational graph.
The key ideas I practiced were defining node-level forward computation, composing a graph evaluator with topological order,
and constructing reverse-mode autodiff by propagating gradients backward through the graph.

I completed the implementation of the missing parts in `auto_diff.py`, including:
- forward `compute` methods for element-wise multiplication, division, scalar variants, and matrix multiplication
- local `gradient` methods for the same operators
- the `Evaluator.run` method for graph execution
- the `gradients` function for backward graph construction

The main difficulty was handling matrix multiplication gradients correctly when transpose flags were enabled.
I solved this by reasoning through the four transpose combinations separately and then validating the formulas with numerical checks
and the provided expected outputs.
